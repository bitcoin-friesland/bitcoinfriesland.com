Frequently Asked Questions

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency created in 2009 by an unknown person under the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. It operates without a central authority or bank, and the network collectively maintains transaction records in a public ledger called the blockchain. Bitcoin can be sent directly from user to user without intermediaries, giving people complete control over their money.

Is Bitcoin sustainable?

Bitcoin mining uses energy, but much of this energy comes from renewable sources. The Bitcoin network encourages innovation in sustainable energy and helps stabilize energy grids by using excess energy.

How do I keep track of my Bitcoin for accounting purposes?

For accounting purposes, you must track all Bitcoin transactions, including date, amount, counterparty, and purpose. Use specialized cryptocurrency accounting software or consult an accountant experienced with digital currencies.

How do I convert Bitcoin to Euros?

You can convert Bitcoin to Euros via cryptocurrency exchanges like Bitvavo, Coinbase, or Kraken. You can also use Bitcoin ATMs or peer-to-peer platforms. Pay attention to exchange rates and transaction fees.

Do I need to register with authorities to use Bitcoin?

In the Netherlands, you don't need to register with authorities to use Bitcoin for personal purposes. However, you must declare Bitcoin profits to the tax authorities. Different rules may apply for commercial activities.

How are Bitcoin profits taxed in the Netherlands?

In the Netherlands, Bitcoin profits are taxed as capital gains (box 3) or as income (box 1) if you trade as an entrepreneur. It depends on your situation and trading frequency. Consult a tax advisor for specific advice.

Isn't Bitcoin too volatile to use as money?

Bitcoin is indeed volatile in the short term, but in the long term it has retained value and grown. Many people use Bitcoin as a store of value (digital gold) rather than a daily payment method.

How do I get started with Bitcoin in Friesland?

Start by downloading a Bitcoin wallet, buy small amounts of Bitcoin via a Dutch exchange like Bitvavo, and join our local Bitcoin community. Participate in our meetups and learn from experienced users.
